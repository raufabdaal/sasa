## Context

Activities of Integration (AOIs) are the heart of CBC assessment. real-world tasks that ask a learner to integrate Knowledge, Skill, Application, and Values in one sustained piece of work. Since 2020, however, **there has been no official bank** of what a good AOI looks like for compulsory Kiswahili. Teachers improvised, and quality varied wildly.

## The Change

NCDC has released **28 official AOIs** spanning S1 through S4, each with a model student response and a per-construct rubric.

## Why

Three years of teacher feedback. The 2024 EPRC survey found AOIs were the most-cited pain point of CBC implementation. Schools without strong master trainers were essentially guessing at the format. A shared standard closes that gap.

## Classroom Example

S2 AOI #7. *"Karibu, Mgeni"*. asks learners to write a 200-word welcome and orientation for a Tanzanian student joining their school. It assesses Knowledge (grammar, vocabulary), Skill (clear written instructions), Application (specific to *their* school), and Values (warmth, honesty about challenges).

## Common Pitfall

**Do not use the model responses as the answer key.** They show *what an A response can look like*. they are not *the* A response. Multiple legitimate responses exist for any AOI. The rubric, not the model, is the source of truth.
