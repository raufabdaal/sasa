## Context

A-Level in Uganda has used a numerical grading scale since the 1970s. **1 (highest) through 9 (lowest)**. with a single composite score per subject. This made comparison easy but hid *what* a learner actually understood.

When the lower-secondary Competency-Based Curriculum landed in 2020, the upper-secondary scale stayed numerical. From 2026, that changes.

## The Change

A-Level joins O-Level on a **letter grading scale of A to E**, with each subject scored across four constructs (Knowledge, Skill, Application, Values) and a final grade composed from the construct grades. not a single number.

| Letter | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| A | Exceptional achievement |
| B | Outstanding performance |
| C | Satisfactory performance |
| D | Basic understanding |
| E | Elementary understanding |

## Why

Two reasons. **Continuity**: a learner who spent O-Level being assessed by construct shouldn't suddenly be reduced to a single number in S5. **Resolution**: a `B` overall that masks an `A` in Application and a `D` in Values tells a teacher more in two letters than a `2` ever did.

## Classroom Example

An S5 Biology learner submits a written-up field study of soil acidity on her family's plot in Mpigi. Old system: she got a `3`. New system: `A` Application, `B` Knowledge, `B` Skill, `C` Values. final grade `B+`, with a clear note that her values discussion (safe handling, neighbor consultation) was thin.

## Common Pitfall

**Do not try to "convert" old numerical marks into letters by a fixed table.** The new system grades constructs separately and then composes. A student with a strong overall numerical 2 might land at `B` because Values pulls them down. Re-grade fresh, not by translation.
