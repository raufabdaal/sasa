Chemistry · S3, S4

Construct weights revised for O-Level Chemistry

Effective July 2026 · NCDC Circular 2026/03 · Reviewed by Aisha Namaganda

Context

When the Lower Secondary Curriculum launched in 2020, each Chemistry assessment was weighted 40% Knowledge, 30% Skill, 30% Application. Values were observed by the teacher but not separately graded.

The Change

From the July 2026 term, the weights are redistributed. Knowledge drops to 30%, the other three constructs balance it, and Values is now graded explicitly at 10%.

Before
Knowledge    40%
Skill        30%
Application  30%
Values   (observed)
After · Jul 2026
Knowledge    30%
Skill        30%
Application  30%
Values       10%

Why

NCDC's 2025 review found learners were over-rewarded for recalling reactions but under-rewarded for applying chemistry to real Ugandan contexts. water treatment, soap-making, fertilizer use.

Lifting Application and naming Values explicitly is meant to bring the test back to the curriculum's original promise.

Classroom Example

An S3 student writes the correct equation for the neutralization of lake-water alkalinity but does not connect it to a community problem. Under old weights: B. Under new weights: C+ overall, with a clear D for Values.

You've got it. Now apply it.
